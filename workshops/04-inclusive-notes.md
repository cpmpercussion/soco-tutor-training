## About this guide

Companion notes to `lectures/04-inclusive.md`. For the **presenter** (running order,
timings, activity setup) and **participants** (what to expect).

The module moves from *concepts* (diversity / inclusion / accessibility, equity vs
equality) → *barriers* → *anti-patterns* → *strategies* → *scenario practice*.

## At a glance

- **Slot:** ~90 minutes (day schedule: 15:30–17:00, the final module).
- **Room:** table groups; sticky notes.
- **Tone:** this is the most sensitive module — model the inclusive, non-judgemental climate you're describing.

## Running order

| Time | Segment | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| ~10 min | Concepts: diversity, inclusion, accessibility; ANU stance; equity vs equality | The equity/equality image lands the core idea. |
| ~4 min | **Task: Diversity in the classroom** (Think-Pair-Share) | 1 min think, 1 min pair, 2 min share. |
| ~8 min | Barriers to learning; accessible digital content | Persona-spectrum framing: permanent / temporary / situational. |
| 5 min | **Task: Identifying barriers** | Sticky notes; find common themes per table. |
| ~5 min | Inclusion anti-patterns intro | "Inclusive teaching is not automatic." |
| 5 min | **Task: Dangerous patterns for inclusion** | Groups name patterns; share one per table. |
| ~8 min | "Heard around ANU" statements + academic standards | Discussion — why might each be problematic? |
| ~10 min | Strategies (UniReady Toolkit's 7 principles) | Focus on what *tutors* can actually influence. |
| ~8 min | **Task: Applying one strategy** | Choose a strategy, concrete actions, benefits/drawbacks. |
| 10+ min | **Scenario discussions** | Scenario 0 for everyone; 1–5 assigned to groups; see below. |

## Facilitation notes

- **"Heard around ANU" statements:** these are deliberately provocative. The aim is
  nuance — several are *defensible* on academic-standards grounds yet still create
  barriers. Avoid a "right answer"; surface the tension.
- **Scenario discussions (Think–Pair–Share, ~10 min each):** run **Scenario 0**
  (stairs / mobility) with everyone first to model the method, then assign
  **Scenarios 1–5** across groups. Each has an **Extension** that raises the stakes
  (peer-rating bias, disclosed autism/anxiety, sexist forum posts, homesickness/
  withdrawal, a transgender student's name) — hold the extension until groups have
  worked the first part.
- These scenarios can surface real experiences. Set expectations for confidentiality
  and respect, and know your referral points (convenor, ANU support services).

## Handoff notes for a new presenter

- This is the **last module of the day** — energy is low; lean on the scenario
  discussions to keep it active rather than adding lecture.
- Pick the scenarios most relevant to your cohort if time is tight — better to do
  two well than five in a rush.

## Materials checklist

- Sticky notes + pens
- Scenarios visible per group (on screen or printed cards)

## Key messages

- **Equity ≠ equality:** students need the support that lets them reach the same outcomes.
- Barriers can be **permanent, temporary, or situational** — inclusive design helps everyone, not only those with a diagnosed need.
- Inclusive teaching is **not automatic**; tutors have real influence through scaffolding, signposting, clear expectations, and knowing where to refer.
