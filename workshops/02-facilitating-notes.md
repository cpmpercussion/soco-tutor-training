## About this guide

Companion notes to `lectures/02-facilitating.md`. For the **presenter** (running
order, timings, activity setup) and **participants** (what to expect).

The through-line: *facilitation* — helping students learn by asking rather than
telling — is the core skill of tutoring. The module builds from teaching theory to
a hands-on role-play.

## At a glance

- **Slot:** ~90 minutes (day schedule: 11:30–13:00, before lunch).
- **Room:** table groups; the final role-play needs **groups of three**.
- **Needs setup before the room:** a PollEverywhere poll and the facilitation video (links below).

## Facilitation notes

- **PollEverywhere activity (A–E → metaphors):** there is no single "correct"
  mapping — the discussion *is* the point. Draw out *why* an activity fits a metaphor.
- **The video** ([Vimeo, ~link in deck](https://vimeo.com/506981439/3fcaf2ac8c)) contrasts an
  *instructor* with a *facilitator*. Have participants watch for what the educator
  actually *does* and *says*.
- **Puzzles role-play (25 min) — the key activity:**
  - Groups of **three**, three rounds, **rotate roles** each round so everyone facilitates once.
  - Roles: **Student** (genuinely stuck), **Facilitator** (helps *only* by asking questions), **Observer** (gives peer feedback after each round).
  - Rounds: Dog Puzzle → Rope Puzzle → Question Puzzle. The **Goat Problem** is an optional fiendish extension for fast groups.
  - Emphasise: the facilitator must resist *telling* — the discomfort of that is the learning.
  - **Don't reveal answers too early**; the struggle models what students feel.

## Key messages

- *"Everything works somewhere; nothing works everywhere."* No teaching approach is correct, singular, or universal.
- **Ask, don't tell** — facilitation questions slow students down, surface resources, and build independence.
- Facilitating well is a **skill that needs practice**, including when you don't know the answer yourself.
