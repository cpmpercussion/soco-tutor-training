## About this guide

Companion notes to `lectures/06-refresher-session.md`. For the **presenter**
(running order, timings, activity setup) and **participants** (what to expect).

This is the **annual refresher** for returning tutors — not the new-starter
induction. It assumes prior teaching experience and mixes admin updates,
reflection, and a strategies workshop.

## At a glance

- **Slot:** ~2 hours (deck schedule: Admin at 00:00, Reflection at 00:30, Strategies at 01:15+).
- **Room:** table groups of 4–5 — ask people to move and re-group at the start.
- **Audience:** experienced tutors; keep admin brisk, invest time in reflection and strategies.

## Running order

| Time | Segment | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| ~5 min | Welcome, Acknowledgement, goals, re-group into tables | |
| 5 min | **Task: Journey as an educator** | Introduce, share what they've taught / will teach. |
| ~20 min | Admin refresher: qualifications, earnings codes, associated working time, contemporaneous marking, safety, help | Flag what's **new** this year (marked ✨ in the deck). |
| 5 min | **Talk: Admin Confusions** | Table discussion of confusing admin; share examples. |
| ~10 min | Kugel's phases of teacher development | Recap from induction; frame the reflection. |
| 10 min | **Task: What stages resonate for you?** | Where are you now on the journey? |
| 5 min | **Task: Joys and Challenges** | Highs/lows; support wanted. |
| ~20 min | High Impact Teaching Strategies (HITS 1–10) | Walk through; connect each to computing. |
| 5 min | **Task: Which strategies resonate?** | Each table picks **one** strategy for the next task. |
| 15 min | **Task: Making concrete plans (Crazy 8s)** | Design sprint; see below. |
| — | Questions / wrap-up | |

## Facilitation notes

- **Admin block:** returning tutors mostly know this — spend the time on the **new
  (✨) items** (qualifications/HESF compliance, contemporaneous marking) and the
  *Admin Confusions* discussion, which is where the real questions surface.
- **Reflection tasks:** the value is peer exchange between experienced tutors —
  keep shares short so several voices are heard.
- **HITS walk-through:** don't lecture all ten evenly; ask the room which they
  already use, and tie *Questioning* and *Feedback* back to the induction modules.
- **Crazy 8s (15 min) — the main activity:**
  - Each table has picked **one** HIT strategy.
  - Fold paper into 8; **8-minute timer**; one sketch of a SoCo application per box; stop on the alarm.
  - Then each person picks their best sketch to present. Keep the timer strict — the constraint is the method.

## Handoff notes for a new presenter

- The **"About Charles" intro slide is commented out** — add your own intro.
- Update the **✨ "new this year"** items so they reflect the current year's changes.
- Bring the **HITS booklet** reference/printout if you want tables to browse it.

## Materials checklist

- Plain paper (one sheet per person) + pens for Crazy 8s
- A visible 8-minute timer
- HITS summary on screen (optionally printed)

## Key messages

- Refreshers are about **peer reflection and improvement**, not repeating induction.
- **High Impact Teaching Strategies** are evidence-backed; tutors can apply many within their agency (esp. questioning, feedback, worked examples, collaborative learning).
- Turn a strategy into a **concrete, sketched plan** you could actually run in a SoCo class.
