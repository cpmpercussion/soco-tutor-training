## About this guide

Companion notes to the slide deck `lectures/01-practicalities.md`. Useful for the
**presenter** (running order, timings, what each activity expects) and for
**participants** (what to expect, and the admin links in one place).

This is the **opening module** of the full induction day, so it also carries the
Acknowledgement of Country, day goals, and schedule.

## At a glance

- **Slot:** ~90 minutes (day schedule: 09:30–11:00, before morning tea).
- **Room:** table groups of 4–5; sticky notes on each table.
- **Two halves:** (1) the *job* — admin, pay, safety; (2) *developing as an educator*.

## Running order

| Time | Segment | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 5 min | Welcome, Acknowledgement, goals, schedule | Set the tone for the whole day. |
| 5 min | **Task: Journey as an educator** | Hands from the room; hear 2–3 prior teaching experiences. |
| ~25 min | The job: qualifications, timesheets, earnings codes, associated working time, contemporaneous marking, 1.5+0.5 structure | The dense admin block — pace it, don't read every link aloud. |
| ~15 min | Safety, emergency contacts, where to get help, working with convenors | Slow down on **safety**: it's the most important slide. |
| 5 min | **Task: Getting to know you as a learner** | 3 sticky notes (enjoy / challenges / support wanted); one of each per table. |
| ~10 min | Developing as a teacher — Kugel's phases (self → subject → student → active → independent → tuning) | Frame as a *journey*, nobody is expected to be at "tuning". |
| ~8 min | **Task: What stages resonate for you?** | Group discussion, then hear responses. Leads into the break. |

## Facilitation notes

- **Admin block is information-dense on purpose** — participants can re-read the links later in this guide. Aim for "here's the shape and where to find it", not memorisation.
- **Safety slide:** state plainly that resolving threats is *not* their job — prioritise personal safety, walk out, call security. Worth pausing for questions.
- **Kugel phases:** the point is reassurance ("teaching is a craft, learned by doing"), not a test. The resonance task works best if you share your own honest answer first.

## Key messages

- You have a **well-defined role** with resources and support — you are not expected to know everything on day one.

- **Safety first**, always. Your convenor is your manager and first point of contact.
